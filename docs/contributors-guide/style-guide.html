Style guide

The style guide acts as a neutral arbiter of 'the right way' to write for this project. This helps to ensure a consistent standard and to minimise edits and rework — which is especially important for a project which will involve multiple authors who work at different times and places.

With proper use, the style guide helps us to:

  • Be consistent. We avoid inconsistencies that confuse or annoy readers.

  • Reduce confrontation, because the authority lies with the style guide. People tend to think that their way of writing something is the best way — but we don’t all agree. The style guide acts as a neutral arbiter of style. This makes it easier for us to set aside personal preferences and biases.

  • Raise the level of trust among contributors. It is easier to collaborate on content when everyone works to the same conventions without heated debate over trivia.

And, writing to a consistent style means:

  • Fewer edits, less rework. We produce high quality content more quickly.

  • Faster onboarding. New contributors can quickly understand and meet expectations.

  • Better content. We avoid ambiguity and common mistakes, so that the publication is easy to understand and free of needless distractions.
